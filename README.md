Dmitry-Grapov
=============
![](https://media.licdn.com/mpr/mpr/shrink_200_200/p/5/005/093/391/0f0497f.jpg)

Dmitry Grapov specializes in data science, bioinformatics and software development. After receiving his B.S. in Biology and Chemistry from the University of Utah (2005, 2007) he went on to finish a Ph.D. in Agricultural and Environmental Chemistry with emphasis in Biotechnology from the University of California, Davis (2012) where his dissertation focused on multivariate data analysis and visualization of quantitative metabolomics and lipidomics methods applied to study the interaction between diet and diseases such as metabolic syndrome and diabetes. Later he went on to become the Principal Statistician at Oliver Fiehn’s West Coast Metabolomics Center, at UC Davis where he developed methods for automated data analysis, reporting and high dimensional network mapping. Currently he is the Visual and Predictive Analytics lead in Genomics and Data Science at Monsanto and is the Director of Data Science and Bioinformatics at CDS- Creative Data Solutions. He has authored > 40 peer reviewed publications over a wide range of topics including metabolomics, lipidomics, glycomics, proteomics, genomics, omics integration, bioinformatics, and software development. When he isn’t hacking away building data analysis and visualization software he enjoys, painting, hiking, mountain biking and spending time with his family.
